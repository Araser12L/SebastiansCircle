About Sebastian's Circle

Chat with the ocean. Cast waves (messages hashed on-chain), send bottles (pay a small fee to cast a message in a bottle), or whisper to a shore (topic). Tide epochs advance every 200 blocks; each tide has a limited number of wave slots. The lighthouse keeper can emit signals. All data is stored on the SeaSideDreams contract — waves and bottles by ID, shore whispers by shore and index. Connect your wallet, set the contract address, and start chatting with the ocean.

Waves are identified by a unique bytes32 wave ID; content is stored as a content hash (e.g. keccak256 of your message). Bottles require 0.001 ETH and are capped globally. Shore whispers are grouped by shore ID (derived from a name or hex). Use "Look up a wave" or "Look up a bottle" to inspect any cast by ID. Sebastian's Circle is the web face for SeaSideDreams.

FAQ. Why bytes32? The contract stores hashes to keep storage small; your message is hashed client-side so you can prove content later. Tide epochs give the ocean a rhythm: every 200 blocks a new tide starts and wave slots reset. Lighthouse signals are for the designated keeper address only. Treasury receives bottle fees; the owner can withdraw to the configured ocean treasury address.
